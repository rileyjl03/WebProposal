Background

Amidst the COVID-19 pandemic, the importance of mental health has only been increasing. Alongside the virus, the stresses and anxiety that we are feeling have also been widespread. The pandemic has been disruptive, not only to our jobs and education, but also our way of life.

From hanging out with friends and family, to playing our favourite sports and going on vacations, the pandemic has placed multiple - and strict - restrictions on our freedom to do as we desire. Many people have lost their jobs. There is uncertainty about how the economy will progress, and when it will recover.

It seems like we may be losing control of where we currently are in our lives. Without a sense of security and a set direction as to how the world is evolving, it is okay to feel anxious, confused, sad or even bored1. These thoughts and feelings can be overwhelming, and we must take the time to look inward and check in with ourselves. We must find support in each other, and reach out to our friends, family, colleagues and even strangers.

Research

Through thorough research, it was observed that there were two main groups that struggled the most with mental health on a day-to-day basis. While the COVID-19 situation may pose impacts on the other age groups as well, it is safe to assume that the effects are felt on top of the circumstances that these two age groups were already facing.

Alongside secondary research on the background of these demographic groups, interviews were also conducted to derive the reasons for psychological pressure during the pandemic, from five individuals from each age group. Questions were also posed to interviewees on the topics they wish to see on the website that aims to provide them with the support they may need.

User Profiles

Through primary and secondary research, characteristics, goals, motivations and frustrations of individuals in the "Young Persons" age group (15 to 24 year olds) and the "Sandwich Generation" (45 to 54 year olds) could be identified. From there, I could create three user profiles that introduce the mental struggles of these parties.

Trevor the Troubled

  • Age: 17
  • Job: Student, Part-time Waiter
  • Family: Older of 2 Siblings, Lives with Parents
  • Location: Sydney, Australia
  • Struggle: Early Onsight of Generalised Anxiety Disorder5, Perfectionism
Background

Trevor is a 17 year old that is in the midst of his final examinations before he moves on to University. On top of his schoolwork, Trevor was working a part-time job as a waiter to earn some pocket money for himself to spend. He likes to exercise and go outdoors - he loves to go on hikes and runs during his free time. He also likes to spend time with friends and family, travelling within and out of the city often.

Struggles

However, Trevor struggles with early onsight of generalised anxiety disorder. He worries about situations that are not within his control or unlikely to happen, with little to no reason to be. He often feels tense. He is also a perfectionist. This means that he expects the best of himself, putting pressure on himself to strive, constantly pushing himself out of his comfort zone. This explains why he is high-functioning, as he believes that being out-and-about and on-the-go with his life is the way to manage his anxiety.

When the COVID-19 pandemic struck, Trevor was forced to learn online, and his job was put on hold. This limited the amount of time he could be out, also varsely decreasing the amount of time he interacted with others. He did not know how to deal with this new plight.

Motivations
  • Better understand his conditions and how to manage them independently
  • Seek professional help
  • Find a good support network where he can speak openly
Frustrations
  • Finding simple and concise information that he can relate to
  • Takes time to open up to his problems
  • Lack of vocabulary to articulate his emotions and thoughts
Preferred Channels
  • Social media - Facebook, Reddit and Snapchat
  • Gaming platform - Discord
  • Mobile - Text and iMessage

Patricia the Parent-Child

  • Age: 50
  • Job: Banker
  • Family: Only child of 2 parents in their late 80s, Wife, Mother of 2 children (Trevor and Tyler), Lives with husband and children
  • Location: Sydney, Australia
  • Struggle: Query Bipolar Disorder6 7, Menopause
Background

Patricia is a 50-year-old mother of 2 children - one who is 17 and one who is 15. She currently resides with her working husband in a suburb in Sydney, Australia, working as a banker. She is also supporting her parents who live further inland, who both work part-time jobs that are still insufficient to support their everyday needs. Therefore, she is considered to be part of the “sandwich generation”, which are a group of individuals who have to earn for their ageing parents and their growing children.

Struggles

Biologically, Patricia is going through menopause. This results in mood swings that affect her feelings, thoughts and behaviour. Additionally, she is suspected to have a bipolar disorder, which causes her to bounce between extreme periods of high energy or self-destructivism. However, while she is aware of her actions, she does not seem to understand why they are so.

When the COVID-19 pandemic struck, Patricia’s job as a banker was threatened. As the economy dipped, she saw more of her colleagues being retrenched, making her feel more anxious about her own position. She starts to observe that the combination of all these struggles have put pressure and inflicted pain onto her husband and children.

Motivations
  • Better understand and cope with her mental and biological conditions
  • Find tips on how to be mindful and calm in situations that are not in her control
  • Find individual and group activities to disengage herself from her stress
Frustrations
  • Lack of time to look through long content
  • Strong belief that there are other worries besides her mental health
  • Struggles with confidence and motivation
Preferred Channels
  • Social media - Facebook and Whatsapp
  • Mobile - Text, Email and iMessage
  • Mail

William the Worried

  • Age: 52
  • Job: Engineer
  • Family: Husband of Patricia and Father of 2 children (Trevor and Tyler)
  • Location: Sydney, Australia
  • Struggle: No queries, Mild periods of insecurity
Background

William is a 52-year-old father to Trevor (and Tyler) and husband of Patricia. Both his parents have passed on when he was younger, so he does not lie within the definition of the “sandwich generation”. He works as an engineer, which requires him to be physically active and on-the-ground on the weekdays. This means that he spends little time at home with his wife and children in the daytime from Monday to Friday.

Struggles

As a third-party, William has observed the changes that have occurred in both his son and wife. Tensions have been rising at home. The two have been struggling to understand each other’s struggles and communicate openly. William feels that it is his responsibility to ensure peace at home. He wants his family to be happy.

Motivations
  • Better understand the struggles of both his son and wife
  • Offer support to both parties by bridging gaps of knowledge and facilitating conversations
  • Peace in the family
Frustrations
  • Difficulty in encouraging the two parties
  • Difficulty in being transparent and open because of the lack of vocabulary
  • Finds himself insecure of his efforts and having the tendency to self-blame
Preferred Channels
  • Mobile - WhatsApp, Text, Email and iMessage
  • Mail
  • News Channels
